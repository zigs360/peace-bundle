# Nigerian User Testing Guide

## Purpose

This guide supports manual user testing for the enterprise UI refresh with participants who reflect Peace Bundle's main audience in Nigeria.

The goal is to validate:

- trust and credibility
- task completion speed
- multilingual clarity
- mobile usability
- comprehension of wallet and VTU flows

## Target Participant Mix

Recruit a balanced mix across:

- students who buy airtime and data frequently
- small business owners
- resellers and agents
- office workers who perform repeat payment tasks
- users comfortable in English
- users who prefer Hausa, Yoruba, or Igbo when available

Recommended minimum:

- 8 to 12 participants
- at least 5 Android-first users
- at least 3 users who primarily use lower-end or mid-range phones

## Devices and Connectivity

Test on conditions that match real usage:

- Android phones with small and medium screens
- one desktop or laptop session
- normal 4G connection
- unstable or slower network simulation when possible

Pay close attention to:

- initial page clarity
- menu discoverability
- wallet and purchase confidence
- page readability under glare and lower brightness

## Core Scenarios

### 1. First Impression

Ask the participant to open the home page and describe:

- what the platform does
- whether it feels trustworthy
- what action they would take first

Success signals:

- user understands airtime, data, wallet, or bill payment purpose quickly
- user notices a clear sign-up or login path
- user describes the UI as professional, safe, or easy to understand

### 2. Language Selection

Ask the participant to switch language and explain what changed.

Success signals:

- language switcher is easy to find
- the participant recognizes their preferred language
- translated labels still feel natural in context

Watch for:

- clipped text
- untranslated labels
- awkward or overly formal wording

### 3. Account Entry

Ask the participant to go through login or registration.

Success signals:

- they understand required versus optional fields
- they can complete the flow without asking what each field means
- they notice support or fallback navigation if they hesitate

### 4. User Dashboard Orientation

Ask the participant to explain:

- wallet balance
- recent activity
- where to buy data
- where to fund wallet

Success signals:

- they identify the main action areas within a few seconds
- they understand card summaries without needing help

### 5. VTU Purchase Confidence

Ask the participant where they would go to buy data or airtime and what details they expect before confirming payment.

Success signals:

- they can find the path quickly
- they expect network, amount, recipient number, and confirmation details
- they feel the interface gives enough confidence before submitting

### 6. Admin or Reseller Workflow

For qualified participants, ask them to inspect a dashboard or operational screen and narrate:

- what each summary metric means
- where they would find plan management or reports
- whether the layout feels manageable

Success signals:

- the hierarchy feels clear
- high-value actions are easy to locate
- the page does not feel visually overwhelming

## Interview Prompts

Use prompts like:

- "What do you think this platform helps you do?"
- "Which button would you tap first?"
- "Does this screen feel safe for money-related actions?"
- "If you wanted to buy data now, where would you go?"
- "Is any wording confusing or unnatural?"
- "Would you use English here, or another language?"
- "What would make this easier on your phone?"

## What to Measure

Track both observation and direct feedback.

### Quantitative

- time to identify primary CTA
- time to find wallet funding
- time to find buy data
- successful language switch completion
- number of hesitations or wrong taps before task completion

### Qualitative

- perceived trustworthiness
- clarity of navigation
- confidence before payment submission
- comfort with translation quality
- readability of cards, menus, and forms

## Nigeria-Specific Checks

Validate these details carefully:

- users understand terms like `wallet`, `airtime`, `data`, `transfer`, and `reseller`
- phone-number entry feels natural
- quick actions match common repeat behaviors
- gold and green accents feel premium, not distracting
- language support improves comfort without causing layout breakage
- mobile navigation works well for one-handed use

## Accessibility Checks During Research

During each session, note whether participants can:

- identify the focused control while tabbing on desktop
- read text comfortably on mobile
- understand icon meaning without relying on color alone
- dismiss menus and drawers easily
- recover from confusion when a screen contains many actions

## Issue Severity Guide

Use this scale when documenting findings:

- Critical: blocks task completion or undermines payment trust
- High: causes repeated confusion, wrong navigation, or missed actions
- Medium: slows the task or weakens confidence
- Low: polish issue that does not materially block use

## Research Output Template

For each session, capture:

- participant type
- device type
- preferred language
- tasks completed
- notable confusion points
- trust comments
- translation comments
- recommended fixes

## Immediate Priorities After Testing

Prioritize findings that affect:

- trust in payment actions
- data and airtime purchase discoverability
- wallet funding clarity
- mobile menu comprehension
- translation quality for top navigation and key CTAs
